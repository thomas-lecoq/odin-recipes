Pizza recipe

Description

A thin, blistered crust brushed with olive oil, topped with bright tomato sauce, melted mozzarella, and fresh basil—simple, balanced, and irresistibly fragrant straight from the oven.

Hints of oregano and garlic elevate every slice. The crisp edge meets creamy cheese. This pizza recipe captures authentic flavor and artisanal mastery.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (flour, water, yeast, salt, olive oil) or ready dough
  • All-purpose or Tipo 00 flour (for dusting)
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic clove (optional for sauce)
  • Crushed tomatoes or pizza sauce
  • Salt
  • Dried oregano
  • Fresh mozzarella (sliced or torn)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Semolina or cornmeal (for stone)

Steps

  1. Prepare dough: mix ingredients, knead, bulk ferment until doubled (1–2 hours) or refrigerate overnight.
  2. Preheat oven and pizza stone to highest possible temperature (250–290°C / 475–550°F).
  3. Make quick sauce: crushed tomatoes, pinch salt, minced garlic and oregano; do not overcook.
  4. Divide dough, rest, then stretch/shape into round on lightly floured surface.
  5. Dust peel with semolina; transfer stretched dough to peel.
  6. Brush rim with olive oil, spread thin layer of sauce, distribute mozzarella evenly.
  7. Slide pizza onto hot stone; bake 7–10 minutes until crust blistered and cheese melted.
  8. Top with fresh basil and a drizzle of olive oil after baking.
  9. Slice and serve immediately.
